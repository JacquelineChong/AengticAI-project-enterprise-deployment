Business Attribute to Prediction Mapping Framework

How to Use This Framework (For Business Users)

This framework helps you document your business knowledge in a way that directly supports the data science team's feature engineering efforts. For each attribute:

  1. Provide specific examples from your business domain
  2. Share sample data or reports that show these attributes in context
  3. Explain business rules that might affect how these attributes should be interpreted
  4. Document your hypotheses about which attributes influence which outcomes

Documentation to Prepare

  1. Data dictionary - defining each business attribute in plain language
  2. Sample reports - showing how you currently analyze these attributes
  3. Business rules document - explaining special cases, calculations, or constraints
  4. Historical examples - describing past patterns you've observed

Sample Data Dictionary Entry

Attribute: Premium Product Affinity Score

Definition: A score from 0-100 indicating a customer's likelihood to purchase premium-tier products

Calculation: Based on previous premium purchases (60%), premium product page views (30%), and email engagement with premium content (10%)

Data Source: Combination of transactional database (Orders table) and web analytics platform

Update Frequency: Calculated weekly, every Sunday at midnight

Segmentation:

  • 0-20: Low premium affinity
  • 21-50: Moderate premium affinity
  • 51-80: High premium affinity
  • 81-100: Very high premium affinity

Business Use: Used to determine eligibility for premium product campaigns and personalized premium recommendations

Sample Business Rules Document

Rule Category: Purchase Frequency Calculation

Rule Description: Special considerations when calculating purchase frequency

Rule Details:

  1. Subscription orders are counted as a single purchase per quarter, regardless of delivery frequency
  2. Returns and cancellations are subtracted from the frequency count
  3. Gift purchases (identified by different shipping address) are weighted at 0.5 of a normal purchase
  4. Seasonal adjustment: November-December purchases receive a 0.8 weight in frequency calculations due to holiday gift buying

Business Rationale: These rules ensure that purchase frequency accurately reflects a customer's true engagement, not just transaction volume.

Example: A customer with 3 subscription deliveries, 2 regular purchases, 1 gift purchase, and 1 return in a quarter would have a calculated frequency of: (3×0.25) + 2 + (1×0.5) - 1 = 2.25

Sample Analysis Example

Analysis Type: Cart Abandonment Pattern Analysis

Key Findings:

  • Cart abandonment rate averages 68% across all product categories
  • Electronics category has highest abandonment (78%) followed by home appliances (72%)
  • Price point correlation: Items >$100 have 2.3x higher abandonment rate than items <$50
  • Time pattern: 65% of abandoned carts occur between browsing and payment information entry
  • When customers return to abandoned carts, 42% complete purchase when offered a 10% discount

Business Hypothesis: Cart abandonment for high-ticket items indicates price sensitivity and research behavior rather than lack of interest. These customers are comparing prices and features across sites.

Observed Patterns: Customers who abandon carts with electronics items often return within 2-3 days, suggesting comparison shopping. Those who do not return within 5 days rarely convert.

Implementation Process

Recommended Workflow for Business Users

  1. Start with high-priority attributes - Focus on the attributes marked as high priority first
  2. Gather existing documentation - Collect any reports, analyses, or definitions you already have
  3. Schedule knowledge transfer sessions - Meet with data scientists to explain your domain knowledge
  4. Document business hypotheses - Explicitly state what you believe drives customer behavior
  5. Provide real examples - Share specific customer scenarios that illustrate your points
  6. Review feature engineering results - Examine how your inputs were translated into model features

Tip: Even incomplete documentation is valuable. Start with what you know best and build from there.
